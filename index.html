System Usage Guide

What is BHIS?

The Built Heritage Intelligence System (BHIS) is a Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) tool built for urban planners, conservation architects, and government entities. It translates raw spatial and structural data into actionable, code-compliant policy interventions.

Workflow & Operations

  1. Data Ingestion: Use the sidebar to ingest ASI (Archaeological Survey of India) or NDMA coordinates. The system automatically structures this data for the engine.
  2. Risk Assessment: The engine processes 7 weighted parameters (Condition, Seismic Zone, Climate Exposure, etc.) to generate a distinct Vulnerability Score V(x) out of 100.
  3. Spatial Context: Click the "GPS" buttons in the output table to instantly verify roofscapes and urban proximity via Google Maps coordinate routing.
  4. Compliance & Export: Review the generated statutory compliance alerts (e.g., AMASR Prohibited Zones). Use the "GeoJSON" button to export the spatial data directly into QGIS/ArcGIS for urban master planning.

Tip: The AHP Engine weights are currently locked to national default thresholds. Future versions will allow dynamic recalibration of W1-W7 for localized district modeling.

Mathematical Methodology & Citations

1. Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (AHP)

The system departs from arbitrary linear weighting and implements an Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) derived from expert pairwise comparison matrices. The vulnerability function $V(x)$ is defined as:

V(x) = f(C_dec, S_rsk, U_prs, C_vul, S_cul, L_prt, E_via)

W_Vector = [0.25, 0.20, 0.15, 0.15, 0.10, 0.10, 0.05]

This allows for the quantification of physical decay against socio-economic viability and legal frameworks.

2. Statutory Compliance Engine

The engine evaluates spatial and legal parameters against Indian National Law:

  • AMASR Act 1958: Auto-flags structures within 100m of ASI protected monuments as "Prohibited Zones", altering retrofit logic to forbid structural expansion.
  • IS 1893 & IS 13935: Triggers specific seismic wrapping and base-isolation recommendations solely when the asset intersects Seismic Zones IV & V coupled with low condition scores.

3. Data Ingestion & Grading Criteria

Synthetic data relying purely on age (`year < 1960`) has been wholly replaced. Identification logic strictly queries:

  • asi_status: Central / State Protected datasets.
  • heritage_grade: INTACH Classification formats (Grade I, II, III).
